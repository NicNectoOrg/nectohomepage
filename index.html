The solution

What you actually need
is an energy strategy.

Solar was the obvious move. It makes sense on almost every roof. But solar is a product - and a product is not a strategy. A strategy starts with understanding your whole energy picture: when you use energy, in what forms, what it's actually costing you, and where it's being wasted.

Most of the businesses I work with have made sound individual investments. The gap is almost never the technology - it's that no one has ever sat down and mapped a staged journey from where they are today to where they need to be, without trying to sell them something at the end of it.

That's the work NECTO does. And the starting point is a 30-minute conversation.

Who this is for

$5M-$50M revenue manufacturer, anywhere in Australia

Senior leadership - MD, Operations Manager, or CFO - who has already made one round of energy investment and needs an independent view on what the next round should look like.

The businesses I work with are typically responding to tenders from organisations at $100M+ revenue - mandatory reporters who are beginning to ask their supply chain about carbon intensity. Your sustainability performance is starting to affect your ability to win work.

  • You manufacture something. The energy complexity that makes this work valuable lives in heat loads, cooling, water systems, and production schedules - not just a lights-and-HVAC bill.
  • You've already started the journey. Solar on the roof, maybe a battery. The first step is done. But the bills haven't come down the way you expected and you're not sure what comes next.

I started my energy career by travelling to Vanuatu to install solar power systems in remote communities. That trip taught me something I've carried through everything since: the point of energy is never the energy itself. It's what it makes possible for people.

Over the past two decades, I've worked across almost every part of the energy sector - wind farm development with Garrad Hassan and Acciona, energy policy for the South Australian Government, international clean energy programs across Southeast Asia and the Pacific as Regional Director for REEEP, and most recently, energy storage technology with Enernet Global and EnerVenue in the US. I've done the work at every level, from installing systems in the field to advising governments on policy to representing Australia at the World Future Energy Summit in Abu Dhabi.

What I kept seeing, across all of it, was the same gap. Businesses that had made smart individual decisions - good equipment, sound investments - but no one had ever helped them see the whole picture. No staged strategy. No view of where the journey was actually heading.

I started NECTO because I wanted to be that person. Not selling a product. Not tied to a vendor. Just genuinely excited about helping manufacturers figure out what their energy needs are, where they want to go and what strategy it will take to get there.

That's what I get to do now. And I love it.
